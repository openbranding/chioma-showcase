# Merchant Intelligence

## Overview

Merchant Intelligence is the operational intelligence layer of Chioma.

It is not a chatbot feature.

Traditional customer support systems wait for customers to ask questions and rely entirely on information manually provided by the business.

Chioma takes a different approach.

Merchant Intelligence continuously evaluates whether a business is truly prepared to serve customers effectively. It identifies missing information, weak operational knowledge, incomplete business policies, support risks, and customer experience gaps before those weaknesses affect customers.

The objective is not merely to answer customer questions.

The objective is to continuously improve the merchant's ability to answer them correctly.

Instead of functioning as a passive support system, Merchant Intelligence acts as an active operational layer that helps merchants strengthen their business readiness, improve support quality, and increase customer confidence.

---

## Why Merchant Intelligence Exists

Most businesses struggle with customer support not because they lack staff, but because they lack structured operational knowledge.

Common problems include:

* Missing business information
* Incomplete product knowledge
* Weak support procedures
* Undefined escalation processes
* Inconsistent customer responses
* Unclear delivery and fulfillment rules
* Poor onboarding processes
* Knowledge scattered across staff members

When these gaps exist, customer experience suffers.

Merchant Intelligence exists to discover these gaps and help merchants close them.

## Core Capabilities

### Knowledge Gap Detection

Chioma identifies missing information required to answer customer questions effectively.

Examples include:

* Missing delivery information
* Missing pricing information
* Missing business policies
* Missing support procedures
* Missing escalation paths
* Missing product knowledge

---

### Merchant Readiness Monitoring

Chioma continuously evaluates how prepared a merchant is to support customers successfully.

Areas evaluated may include:

* Product readiness
* Support readiness
* Knowledge readiness
* Escalation readiness
* Commerce readiness
* Communication readiness

---

### Business Policy Governance

Chioma identifies situations where important business policies are missing, incomplete, or unclear.

Examples include:

* Refund policies
* Delivery policies
* Support policies
* Escalation procedures
* Payment procedures

---

### Customer Experience Risk Detection

Chioma detects weaknesses that may negatively impact customer experience.

Examples include:

* Frequently unanswered questions
* Missing business information
* High escalation rates
* Knowledge coverage gaps
* Inconsistent responses

---

### Guided Knowledge Collection

Chioma guides merchants through structured questions that improve business knowledge coverage.

Instead of expecting merchants to know what information is missing, Chioma actively helps them discover and document it.

---

### Operational Recommendations

Chioma proposes improvements that help merchants strengthen customer engagement and business operations.

Examples include:

* Filling knowledge gaps
* Improving FAQ coverage
* Strengthening escalation processes
* Improving readiness scores
* Reducing customer support risks

---

### Commerce Readiness Evaluation

Chioma evaluates whether a business is operationally prepared to handle customer purchases, inquiries, fulfillment requests, and support interactions.

---

### Support Quality Improvement

Chioma continuously identifies opportunities to improve customer support quality through better knowledge, better processes, and better operational readiness.

---

### Continuous Business Intelligence

Merchant Intelligence is not a one-time assessment.

It continuously monitors business readiness and operational quality, helping merchants improve over time.
