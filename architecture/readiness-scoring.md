# Readiness Scoring

## Overview

Readiness Scoring helps Chioma measure how prepared a business is to support customers, answer questions, process requests, and operate effectively.

The score is not intended to judge a business. It is intended to identify opportunities for improvement.

---

## Purpose

The readiness score helps:

- Identify operational gaps
- Improve customer experience
- Increase response quality
- Reduce support escalations
- Improve merchant confidence

---

## Readiness Categories

### Business Information

Measures availability and quality of:

- Company information
- Contact information
- Business hours
- Locations
- Policies

---

### Product Knowledge

Measures availability and quality of:

- Product catalog
- Product descriptions
- Pricing information
- Inventory information
- Product FAQs

---

### Customer Support

Measures availability and quality of:

- Support channels
- Escalation paths
- Response procedures
- Customer assistance information

---

### Commerce Operations

Measures availability and quality of:

- Ordering process
- Delivery information
- Payment information
- Refund policies
- Fulfillment procedures

---

### Knowledge Coverage

Measures overall completeness of merchant knowledge.

Examples:

- Frequently asked questions
- Business rules
- Operational guidance
- Customer communication content

---

## Score Interpretation

### Excellent

Merchant information is complete and well maintained.

### Good

Minor improvements recommended.

### Fair

Important gaps identified.

### Needs Improvement

Significant operational gaps detected.

---

## Continuous Evaluation

Readiness is continuously reviewed as business information changes.

New gaps and improvement opportunities may affect future readiness assessments.

---

## Business Value

Readiness Scoring provides a measurable framework for improving merchant operational quality and customer engagement outcomes.

---

## Status
