# Conversation Learning Workflow

## Objective

Show how Chioma continuously learns from customer conversations while keeping merchant knowledge controlled and approved.

---

## Why This Workflow Exists

Customer conversations contain valuable business intelligence.

Repeated questions, unanswered requests, missing policies, unclear product information, and recurring support issues reveal opportunities to improve business readiness.

Chioma identifies these opportunities and proposes improvements without automatically changing merchant knowledge.

---

## Learning Lifecycle

```text
Customer Conversation
        ↓
Conversation Analysis
        ↓
Pattern Detection
        ↓
Knowledge Gap Identification
        ↓
Improvement Suggestions Generated
        ↓
Merchant Review
        ↓
Merchant Approval
        ↓
Knowledge Base Updated
        ↓
Future Customer Responses Improved
```

---

## Step 1: Customer Conversation

Customers interact with the business through supported communication channels.

Examples:

- Product questions
- Pricing requests
- Delivery inquiries
- Support requests
- Business policy questions

---

## Step 2: Conversation Analysis

Chioma analyzes conversations to understand:

- Frequently asked questions
- Missing information
- Incomplete responses
- Escalation patterns
- Customer frustration signals

---

## Step 3: Pattern Detection

Chioma identifies recurring issues.

Examples:

- Customers repeatedly ask for delivery timelines
- Customers ask about payment methods
- Customers request unavailable product details
- Customers ask questions that staff answer inconsistently

---

## Step 4: Knowledge Gap Identification

Chioma compares customer needs against approved merchant knowledge.

Potential gaps may include:

- Missing FAQs
- Missing policies
- Missing catalog information
- Missing operational procedures
- Missing customer support guidance

---

## Step 5: Improvement Suggestions

Chioma generates draft recommendations.

Examples:

- Add delivery policy
- Add return policy
- Add product specifications
- Improve support responses
- Expand business information

These recommendations remain drafts.

---

## Step 6: Merchant Review

The merchant reviews all proposed improvements.

The merchant can:

- Approve
- Edit
- Reject
- Postpone

---

## Step 7: Merchant Approval

Only approved knowledge becomes available for future customer engagement.

No business information is changed automatically.

---

## Step 8: Continuous Improvement

As more conversations occur:

- Readiness improves
- Knowledge becomes more complete
- Customer responses become more consistent
- Operational gaps become easier to identify

---

## Governance Principle

Chioma learns from conversations.

Chioma proposes improvements.

The merchant remains the final authority over business knowledge.
