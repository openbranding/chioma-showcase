# Knowledge Approval Workflow

## Objective

Ensure that Chioma only uses approved merchant knowledge when supporting customers.

---

## Why This Workflow Exists

Business information must remain accurate, controlled, and merchant-owned.

Chioma may detect missing or weak knowledge, but it should not silently change what the business says to customers.

---

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Knowledge Gap Detected

Chioma identifies missing, unclear, outdated, or incomplete business information.

Examples:

- Missing delivery policy
- Unclear refund rule
- Incomplete product details
- Weak FAQ answer
- Missing escalation instruction

### Step 2: Draft Improvement Created

Chioma prepares a suggested knowledge update for the merchant to review.

The draft may include:

- Suggested FAQ answer
- Improved product description
- Policy clarification
- Customer support response
- Operational instruction

### Step 3: Merchant Review

The merchant reviews the proposed knowledge update before it becomes active.

The merchant can:

- Approve
- Edit
- Reject
- Ask for revision

### Step 4: Approved Knowledge Becomes Active

Only approved knowledge is used in customer conversations.

Rejected or unapproved drafts must not be used as official business information.

### Step 5: Knowledge History is Preserved

Chioma keeps a record of knowledge changes for traceability and business confidence.

---

## Governance Rules

- Merchant knowledge belongs to the merchant.
- Chioma must not silently overwrite business information.
- Draft knowledge is not active knowledge.
- Customer-facing answers should rely on approved knowledge.
- Sensitive or unclear issues should be escalated.

---

## Business Value

This workflow helps merchants maintain accurate communication, reduce wrong answers, and improve customer trust.

---

## Status

Planned and evolving as part of Chioma merchant intelligence and knowledge governance.
